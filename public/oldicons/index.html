Windows 9X Icons

As you may have noticed by the overall design of this website, I’m a fan of retro UI design. Putting aside arguments such as better legibility and performance, there’s just something about them, perhaps the low resolution of assets that make every pixel discernible, that makes you aware that you are indeed using a computer.

A big part of computer UI design are icons - for which this page is a sort of museum. The constraints of limited color depth and resolution that designers were burdened with in the 1990s led to more creativity and rawness. Something definitely worth mentioning is Susan Kare's UI graphics work for Apple, which perfectly showcase how technical constraints led to the creation of no-nonsense, recognizable and iconic icons.

These icons have been extracted from a large number of Windows 9X era apps. I've tried to reduce the number of duplicate icons to a minimum, though some may still be present.
